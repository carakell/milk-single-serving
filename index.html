With six grams of protein per ounce, 12 different vitamins and minerals and a high amount of good-for-you monounsaturated fat, almonds have a myriad of nutritional benefits. They also happen to be a good source of magnesium.

Coconuts are high in healthy saturated fats that have different effects than most other fats in your diet. These fats can boost fat burning and provide your body and brain with quick energy.

Many people consider hemp seeds to be a superfood. The seeds have a rich nutritional profile and provide a range of health benefits. Although hemp seeds come from the Cannabis plant, they do not produce a mind-altering effect. They are rich in protein, fiber, and healthful fatty acids.

Hazelnuts are rich in unsaturated fats, high in magnesium, calcium and vitamin B. Hazelnuts are good for your heart, help reduce the risk of cancer, and aid in muscle, skin, bone, joint and digestive health.

Oats are a very good source of fiber, and are high in vitamins, minerals and antioxidants. They have protective effects against heart disease, lower blood sugar and cholesterol levels, and provide easy energy.

Not only do cashews have a lower fat content than most other nuts, approximately 82% of their fat is unsaturated fatty acids, plus about 66% making them heart-healthy. They are also extremely high in protein and vitamin E!
